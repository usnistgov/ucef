Upcoming Webinar: Universal CPS Environment for Federation (UCEF) GridLAB-D Federate (November 15, 2018, 12:00 p.m. EDT)

We will conduct a webinar introducing the UCEF GridLAB-D Federate this coming Thursday as the next in a series of presentations on components of the UCEF platform. The GridLAB-D federate is a “wrapper” java library that can be used to integrate a the GridLAB-D simulator developed by Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) into the High Level Architecture (HLA) platform used for UCEF. GridLAB-D is a powerful simulator of simple and complex energy grid simulations. This webinar will show how to use this component in UCEF simulations and how to cause data to be extracted from and injected into a GridLAB-D model. Please join us to learn about this tool and how it can be utilized to bring any domain-specific framework into the UCEF federated testbed environment.

This is the next in a series of presentations held the third Thursday of each month, 12:00 P Eastern Time, Beginning October 18. Join the webinar at: https://global.gotomeeting.com/join/832557933.  Phone: 1(408)650-3131;32557933#.

These components are part of the UCEF 1.0.0 BETA Platform and will be the topics of the monthly webinars in order:

  • UCEF Gateway – rapid development adaptor to UCEF Platform
  • GridLAB-D™ – Powerful re-usable Federate based on ucef-gateway to integrate PNNL Power System simulator
  • Database – UCEF database federate and tools to automate software-less data capture from federated experiments
  • LabVIEW™ – Integrate hardware and simulations using National Instruments LabVIEW platform, a re-usable federate based on the ucef-gateway
  • Courses of Action (COA) – graphical experiment orchestration language in WebGME

UCEF is an open-source tool kit that provides a collaborative experiment-development environment.

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and its partner, the Institute for Software Integrated Systems at Vanderbilt University, have developed a collaborative experiment development environment across heterogeneous architectures integrating best-of-breed tools including programming languages, communications co-simulation, simulation platforms, hardware in the loop, and others. This environment combines these simulators and emulators from many researchers and companies with a standardized communications protocol, IEEE Standard 1516 High Level Architecture (HLA).
